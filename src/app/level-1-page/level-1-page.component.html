Level 1

Word → target case

Each question has three fields. In the first one you will be presented with your base word. In the second field there are the target case and number. And you must input your answer in the third field, which consist of the base word, declined/conjugated with respect to the information given to you in the second field.

Click on the settings icon to change localizations, appearance, typography and such options. Click on the question mark to get more information on cases, what they mean, when they're used and so on. If you get stuck you can tap on "Hint" under the input field to see the gender of the word or the conjucation table.

You may write your answers either in normal or etymological orthographies, cyrillic or latin, as you wish. But please note that incorrect usage of the letters “z”, “c”, “s” as opposed to “ž”, “č”, “š” will be deemed an error. If you are having trouble inputting these characters, you can find a list of Windows, MacOS, and GBoard layouts here. Or you can use the on-screen keyboard by pressing the button in the corner.

For Linux users (specifically of Ubuntu-based distros and/or using X11) — stay tuned, I'm working on a project just for you.
