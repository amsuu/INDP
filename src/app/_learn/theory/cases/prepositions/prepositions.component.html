Prepositions

With Genitive

Prepositions are used, which imply movement away or out of a location.
(And really, that just happens to include the better part of all the prepositions in Interslavic.)

  • iz – from, out of
  • iz-medžu – from between
  • iz-nad – from above
  • iz-pod – from under
  • iz-prěd – from before
  • iz-srěd – from among
  • iz-za – from behind
  • od – of, from (away from); since; by (agent)
  • u – at, at the place of

Which includes prepositions for relative location,

  • blizko – near, close to
  • kolo – next to, around
  • naprotiv – opposite
  • okolo – around; about, approximately
  • podle – near, beside
  • pored – next to
  • posrěd – amidst, in the middle of, among
  • povrh – to the upper side of, atop
  • srěd – among, amid
  • vně – outside of
  • vnutri – inside, within

For movement relative to the object,

  • mimo – past, by
  • poprěk – across
  • vdolž – along

For time,

  • podčas – during, so long as
  • poslě – after
  • za – during, in times of

And some other miscellaneous grammatical prepositions:

  • bez – without
  • dlja – for
  • do – to, till
  • kromě – except
  • odnosno – concering, about
  • pomimo – in spite of, despite
  • posrědstvom – by means of, using
  • suprotiv – contrary to
  • vměsto – instead of
  • vslěd – following, because of, as a result of

With Accusative

Prepositions are used, which imply movement towards/into/through a location:

  • črěz, prěz – through, across, via
  • po – for (in pursuit of, to get something); for (the Xth time); up to
  • za – for, because of, due to, in exchange for, in favour of, instead of

With Dative

Prepositions, implying the action is directed towards something/someone
(similarly to the meaning of the case itself)
(And if you'll notice, most of the English translations end in “to” as well)

  • blagodare, dekujuči, hvala – thanks to
  • k, ko – to, towards (direction)
  • po – according to, per, by
  • po – for ... each, for ... a piece
  • protiv – against
  • soglasno, suglasno – according to

With Instrumental

Similar to the meaning of the case itself, the prepositions imply a method or accompaniment.

  • s, so – with, together with; by means of, using
  • ponad – above, beyond

With Locative

The Locative is used with the following prepositions:

  • o, ob – about; concerning, regarding
  • po – after, following; along(side); throughout, round, all over
  • pri – near; during (rule of a king)

With Accusative and Instrumental

There are five prepositions for place (and time) in Interslavic, which can take either the Accusative or Instrumental case. Which case is used depends on the meaning:

The Instrumental is used when the location is stable. For example:

Kot spi pod stol.
The cat is sleeping under the table. (Instrumental)

And the Accusative is used when there is an implied movement to or from the location. Like:

Kot poběgl pod stolom.
The cat fled to under the table. (Accusative)

The prepositions are:

  • medžu – between (place & time)
  • nad – above, over, beyond
  • pod – under
  • prěd – before, in front of, ahead of (place & time), prior to, ago
  • za – behind (place), after (time)

With Locative and Accusative

The below two prepositions work the same way as the above, only that (more logically) the Locative is used instead of the Accusative when the location is stable.

Kot spi na stolu
The cat is sleeping on the table (Locative)

Kot poběgl na stol
The cat fled onto the table (Accusative)

The prepositions are:

  • na (+ Locative) – on, at
  • v, vo (+ Locative) – in, within
  • na (+ Accusative) – onto, unto
  • v, vo (+ Accusative) – into

Prepositional constructions

These are not prepositions in and of themselves, but rather constructions of words, usually requiring a special case (similarly to prepositions), which are often used (and treated) as a preposition.

To give a similar example in English: The phrases “with regards to” or “with the help of”. They aren't prepositions, but usually are treated as one singular block.

And if you think about it, those also require a special case in English, when it comes to pronouns:
“with regards to him”, not
“with regards to he”

And like before there are a couple of different case uses, depending on the construction. (Don't worry, they all make sense). So let's look through them:

Genitive

These are once again, with the Genitive, most of the constructions in Interslavic.

Here the important thing to remember, to keep an intuition for the cases, is the correspondance between the English “of” and the Interslavic Genitive case. (Remember this icon? table_lamp) And once again, notice that most of the English translations end in “of”.

  • s izključenjem – except, with the exception of
  • s pomočju – with the help of
  • v imenu – in the name of
  • v obličju – in the face of
  • v smyslu – in the sense of
  • v času – during (Lit. “In the hour of”)
  • v těčenju – during (Lit. “In the flow of”)
  • v vrěmenu – during (Lit. “In the time of”)
  • čto se tyče – regarding, with regard to
  • pod vodstvom – under leadership of
  • na prěkor – in defiance of
  • od strany – by, on the part of
  • v ramah, v ramkah – within the framework of, as a part of
  • v odrazlikě od – unlike, in contrast to
  • v različju od – unlike, in difference to
  • susedno do – next to, neighbouring to

The last three have the Genitive simply because the last word of the construction is a preposition requiring the Genitive. I've marked this with italics.

Accusative

Similarly, the following constructions are used with Accusative, simply because of the ending preposition. This pattern holds for Dative and Instrumental so I won't repeat it again in those places.

  • bez obzira na – regardless
  • s obzirom na – with regard to
  • v zaměn za – instead of, in exchange for
  • ne gledajuči na – disregarding, despite, in spite of, notwithstanding

Dative

  • po odnošenju k – regarding, with regard to
  • v odnošenju k – regarding, with regard to
  • v protivnosti k – unlike, in opposition to

Instrumental

  • sgodno s – conforming with
  • v svezi s – in connection with
