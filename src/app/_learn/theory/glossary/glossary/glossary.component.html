Common English (Latin) shortenings

These are shortenings or abbreviations, which are very common in any English text, and so these aren't exclusive only to this blog. They most comonly come from latin (which I've shown with lat.) because... well... 18th century English scholars were massive fanboys for the completely dead language Latin... What can you do.

  • etc. – and so on (lat. et cetera)
  • e.g. – for example (lat. exempli gratia)
  • i.e. – that is... (lat. id est)
  • c., ca. – around (lat. circa)
  • et al. – and the others (lat. et alli)

  • sic – quoted as-is, the mistake is part of the original (lat. sic)
